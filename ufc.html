/ UFC 142

/ Concept Adaptation and Development / Creative Direction / Key Visual / Event Look&Feel / Signage System / Marketing Materials Development / Media Creation & Execution / Scenery Creation & Supervision

RIO DE JANEIRO, BRAZIL, 2012.

The fastest growing sports organization in the world, the Ultimate Fighting Championship® (UFC®), started in 1993 as a professional mixed martial arts (MMA) organization. UFC has revolutionized the fight business, and today stands as the world's leading MMA promoter, offering the premier series of MMA sports events that have sold out some of the biggest arenas and stadiums across the globe.

The UFC 142, also called as UFC Rio 2, was the second edition of the event in Rio de Janeiro and it was held in HSBC Arena for an audience of more than 12,000 spectators. Aldo vs Mendes was the main fight and marked the apogee of the brazilian fighter, who climbed the crowd after having won the fight.

Inside and outside of the office.

IMX (former IMM name) was the local producer for the first seven UFC events in Brazil including UFC 142. The in house creative department took resposabilities in two main roles.

For the promotion and scenery materials, layouts were developed starting from styleguides received from Las Vegas UFC headquarters. As expected, not all situations were covered by the styleguides, so the opportunity was given to create and suggest some new artwork, that were then approved.

However, the signage project was created from ground zero. Together with event producers, dozens of visits were done to understand audience flow and suggest the best solutions.

The challenge was made, and that fight has been won.

Designers: Daniel Viana, Marcelo Araujo, Luciano Figueiredo, Julia Goulart, Fernanda Colaço (intern).
