/ RIO Open

/ Creative Direction / Key Visual / Event Look&Feel / Concept Development / Team Management / Media Creation & Execution / Signage System / Scenery Creation & Supervision / Presentation Design

RIO DE JANEIRO, BRAZIL, 2018.

Rio Open is the first ATP World Tour 500 tournament in Brazilian history. It is the most important annual sporting event in the city of Rio de Janeiro, and one of the few ATP 500 tournaments played on clay.

Some of the greatest tennis stars were part of the tournament: Rafael Nadal, David Ferrer, Jo-Wilfried Tsonga, Gael Monfils, Marin Cilic, Dominic Thiem, John Isner and Fabio Fognini.

In addition to nine clay courts, including the Guga Kuerten Center Court with a 6,200-seat capacity, there are Leblon Boulevard, an area with stands, stores and food courts for the audience and the Corcovado Club, an exclusive VIP area.

The fifth edition of the biggest tennis tournament in South America received 48,000 spectators in 2018 at the Jockey Club Brasileiro. It was a remarkable milestone for both the city and the tournament.

The challenge. The inspiration.

In its fifth edition, it is a challenge for an event to reinvent itself, maintaining and enhancing the pros and avoiding the cons to remain relevant to the community.

This time the story was told from the audience point of view. The audience that thrill, have fun, share the moment and help write their names in Rio Open's clay.

Visually, this year was paid a homage to the clay, a surface that represents the soul of Rio Open. Art was one of the four experience pillars of the tournament, and clay did turn into an artistic element in all of the event look and feel. Abstract, artistic and conceptualised at the same time.

Designers: Daniel Viana, Luciano Figueiredo, Lucas Gomes, Marcelo Rampim, Jessica Marins (intern), Laura Gama, Marcus Schaeffer.
